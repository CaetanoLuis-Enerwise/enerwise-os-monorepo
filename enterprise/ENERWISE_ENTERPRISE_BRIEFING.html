PILOT DESIGN

Progressive risk reduction with explicit gates.

0. Qualification - 3 to 5 days

Data sample, tariff, topology, equipment, value estimate, integration gaps, go/no-go.

1. Data and Baseline - 1 to 2 weeks

Read-only ingestion, data quality, bill reconstruction, KPI freeze, baseline acceptance.

2. Shadow Operations - 4 to 6 weeks

Forecast and schedule every 30 minutes without hardware control. Measure forecast error, hypothetical value, availability, throughput, and exceptions.

3. Controlled Dispatch - 2 to 4 weeks

Authenticated adapter, restricted envelope, command acknowledgement, telemetry confirmation, fallback, emergency stop, reviewed expansion.

4. Production Decision

Accept, extend, remediate, or stop based on jointly agreed technical, safety, security, and economic gates.

Pilot statement of work5
SECURITY AND SAFETY

Closed-loop control is an acceptance milestone, not a demo feature.

Required Controls

  • TLS, workload identity, RBAC, and rotated secrets.
  • Immutable schedule, command, acknowledgement, and override audit.
  • Fresh telemetry and device-health validation.
  • Power, SoC, reserve, time-validity, and emergency-stop interlocks.
  • Local-controller fallback and tested rollback.

Current Production Blockers

  • No customer-specific device adapter.
  • No customer-device command acknowledgement loop.
  • No production identity/authorization deployment.
  • No external immutable production audit destination.
  • No hardware-in-the-loop or site commissioning evidence.
  • No completed customer security assessment.
These blockers do not prevent read-only integration or shadow mode. They deliberately prevent unsupported unattended physical control.
