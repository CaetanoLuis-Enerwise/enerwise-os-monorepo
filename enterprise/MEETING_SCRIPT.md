# Enterprise Meeting Script

## Opening

"Enerwise is a predictive battery operations layer. It combines site
consumption, PV, tariffs, and battery constraints to create a new dispatch
plan every 30 minutes. We are proposing a measured shadow-mode pilot, not an
unverified production claim."

## Discovery Questions

1. Which energy problem has executive priority?
2. How is the battery controlled today?
3. Which data is available and at what interval?
4. What are the tariff and demand-charge mechanisms?
5. Which inverter/BMS protocols are available?
6. Who owns cybersecurity and site safety approval?
7. What financial threshold would justify rollout?

## Evidence

"Our current reproducible benchmark covers 21,936 real observations. The
120 kWh scenario increased solar self-consumption from 87.67% to 98.52% and
reduced grid import by 16.3 MWh. It did not reduce the site's maximum monthly
peak. We show both results because the pilot must identify where value really
exists for your site."

## Ask

"We need a selected site, 6-12 months of interval data, tariff details,
equipment specifications, and named technical/security owners. We will first
reconstruct the baseline and issue a go/no-go report. Hardware control only
starts after shadow-mode and formal safety acceptance."

## Avoid

- Do not say "guaranteed savings".
- Do not call dry-run physical production.
- Do not claim peak reduction when evidence shows none.
- Do not promise support for an inverter before protocol validation.
- Do not expose local repository disorder or research artefacts in meetings.
