# Enerwise Commercial Model

Internal working document. Final pricing must follow customer qualification,
integration discovery, procurement requirements, and legal review.

## Qualification Rule

Do not sell closed-loop implementation before the data assessment demonstrates
a credible value pool.

Recommended gate:

- three-year expected gross value should exceed total implementation and
  operating cost by at least 3x; or
- the customer identifies strategic value such as fleet learning, resilience,
  regulatory readiness, or multi-site rollout that justifies the pilot.

## Indicative Engagement Bands

### Data and Value Assessment

EUR 5,000-10,000.

Includes data quality analysis, tariff reconstruction, historical simulation,
technical discovery, and a written go/no-go report.

### Shadow-Mode Pilot

EUR 15,000-30,000.

Includes live read-only ingestion, forecasting, scheduling, dashboard,
monitoring, weekly review, and final KPI report.

### Controlled Hardware Pilot

Additional EUR 20,000-50,000.

Depends on vendor protocol, gateway requirements, cybersecurity controls,
commissioning, hardware-in-the-loop testing, and on-site support.

### Production

Price after the pilot using one of:

- annual platform fee per site;
- platform fee plus managed operations;
- fleet license;
- base fee plus a share of independently verified savings.

Avoid pure savings-share contracts until baseline ownership, tariff changes,
weather normalization, production changes, and measurement disputes are
contractually defined.

## Cost-Based Minimum

Calculate the commercial floor from:

- solution engineering days;
- data engineering days;
- device adapter development;
- security and procurement effort;
- commissioning and support;
- cloud and observability costs;
- warranty/incident risk;
- target gross margin.

Never price solely from the demonstration dataset's savings. Enterprise price
must reflect integration scope and fleet value while preserving a credible
customer payback.

## Customer-Facing Commercial Principles

- Qualification before commitment.
- Fixed scope and acceptance criteria.
- Change control for additional devices/sites.
- No guaranteed savings before customer measurement.
- Customer owns site safety and authorizes physical control.
- Production SLA begins only after formal acceptance.
